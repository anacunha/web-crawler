   <h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">Renewable energy in Norway</h1> 
   <div id="bodyContent" class="mw-body-content"> 
    <div id="siteSub">
     From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia
    </div> 
    <div id="contentSub"></div> 
    <div id="jump-to-nav" class="mw-jump">

    <div id="mw-content-text" lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr">
     <div class="thumb tright"> 
      <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">
       <a href="/wiki/File:Storvassdammen_3.JPG" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/db/Storvassdammen_3.JPG/220px-Storvassdammen_3.JPG" width="220" height="147" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/db/Storvassdammen_3.JPG/330px-Storvassdammen_3.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/db/Storvassdammen_3.JPG/440px-Storvassdammen_3.JPG 2x" data-file-width="4272" data-file-height="2848"></a> 
       <div class="thumbcaption"> 
        <div class="magnify">
         <a href="/wiki/File:Storvassdammen_3.JPG" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a>
        </div> The 
        <a href="/wiki/Ulla-F%C3%B8rre" title="Ulla-Førre">Ulla-Førre</a> hydropower complex has an installed capacity of approximately 2,100 MW
       </div> 
      </div> 
     </div> 
     <p><a href="/wiki/Norway" title="Norway">Norway</a> is a heavy producer of <a href="/wiki/Renewable_energy" title="Renewable energy">renewable energy</a>, first of all due to good resources in <a href="/wiki/Hydropower" title="Hydropower">hydropower</a>. Over 99% of the electricity production in mainland Norway is covered by hydropower plants. The total production of electricity from hydropower plants amounted to 135.3 TWh in 2007<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> There is also a large potential in <a href="/wiki/Wind_power" title="Wind power">wind power</a>, <a href="/wiki/Offshore_wind_power" title="Offshore wind power">offshore wind power</a><sup id="cite_ref-nvesea_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nvesea-2">[2]</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/Wave_power" title="Wave power">wave power</a>, as well as production of <a href="/wiki/Bio-energy" title="Bio-energy" class="mw-redirect">bio-energy</a> from wood.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> Norway has limited resources in solar energy, but is one of the world's largest producers of <a href="/wiki/Solar_grade_silicon" title="Solar grade silicon" class="mw-redirect">solar grade silicon</a> and silicon <a href="/wiki/Solar_cells" title="Solar cells" class="mw-redirect">solar cells</a>.</p> 
     <p><a href="/wiki/Guarantee_of_origin" title="Guarantee of origin">Guarantees of Origin</a>, the system for Guarantees of Origin was implemented by the EU Renewable Energy <a href="/wiki/Directive_2009/28/EC" title="Directive 2009/28/EC" class="mw-redirect">Directive 2009/28/EC</a>. In 2010 the average electricity consumption mix of a Norwegian household was 36% renewable.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>

     <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Wind_power">Wind power</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Renewable_energy_in_Norway&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Wind power">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
     <div class="thumb tright"> 
      <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">
       <a href="/wiki/File:Vallersund_handelssted.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2f/Vallersund_handelssted.jpg/220px-Vallersund_handelssted.jpg" width="220" height="147" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2f/Vallersund_handelssted.jpg/330px-Vallersund_handelssted.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2f/Vallersund_handelssted.jpg/440px-Vallersund_handelssted.jpg 2x" data-file-width="3888" data-file-height="2592"></a> 
       <div class="thumbcaption"> 
        <div class="magnify">
         <a href="/wiki/File:Vallersund_handelssted.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a>
        </div> Wind turbine in 
        <a href="/wiki/Bjugn" title="Bjugn">Bjugn</a>
       </div> 
      </div> 
     </div> 
     <p>In 2012 Norway had a <a href="/wiki/Wind_power" title="Wind power">wind power</a> electricity production of 1.6 GWh. It aims to triple its capacity of currently ca.700 MW to 2GW by 2020.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p> 
     <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Transport">Transport</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Renewable_energy_in_Norway&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Transport">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
     <div class="thumb tright"> 
      <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">
       <a href="/wiki/File:Buddy_black.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e0/Buddy_black.jpg/220px-Buddy_black.jpg" width="220" height="152" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e0/Buddy_black.jpg/330px-Buddy_black.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e0/Buddy_black.jpg/440px-Buddy_black.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1623" data-file-height="1118"></a> 
       <div class="thumbcaption"> 
        <div class="magnify">
         <a href="/wiki/File:Buddy_black.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a>
        </div> 
        <a href="/wiki/Buddy_(electric_car)" title="Buddy (electric car)">Buddy</a>, an electric car produced in Norway
       </div> 
      </div> 
     </div> 
     <p>In the transport sector the share of renewables has increased from 1.3% to 4% between 2005-2010, and currently Norway has one of the highest numbers of <a href="/wiki/Electric_car" title="Electric car">electric cars</a> per capita in the world.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
