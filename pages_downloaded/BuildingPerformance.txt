   <h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">Building performance</h1> 
   <div id="bodyContent" class="mw-body-content"> 
    <div id="siteSub">
     From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia
    </div> 
    <div id="contentSub"></div> 
    <div id="jump-to-nav" class="mw-jump">
      Jump to: 
     <a href="#mw-head">navigation</a>, 
     <a href="#p-search">search</a> 
    </div> 
    <div id="mw-content-text" lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr">
     <p><b>Building performance</b> or <b>home performance</b> is a comprehensive whole-house approach to identifying and fixing comfort and <a href="/wiki/Efficient_energy_use" title="Efficient energy use">energy efficiency</a> problems in the built environment.<sup id="cite_ref-Wheeler_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Wheeler-1"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup></p> 
     <p><a href="/wiki/Energy_audit" title="Energy audit">Energy audits</a> are performed by energy auditors, also known as building analysts or consultants,<sup id="cite_ref-Consultantmodel_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Consultantmodel-2"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup> which, in the United States, are usually certified by the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Building_Performance_Institute&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Building Performance Institute (page does not exist)">Building Performance Institute</a> (BPI) or the Residential Energy Services Network (<a href="/w/index.php?title=RESNET&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="RESNET (page does not exist)">RESNET</a>).<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Southface_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Southface-4"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup></p> 
     <p>The United States <a href="/wiki/United_States_Environmental_Protection_Agency" title="United States Environmental Protection Agency">Environmental Protection Agency</a> and <a href="/wiki/United_States_Department_of_Energy" title="United States Department of Energy">Department of Energy</a> started a national program "<i>Home Performance with <a href="/wiki/Energy_Star" title="Energy Star">ENERGY STAR</a></i>" to offer a comprehensive, whole-house approach to improving energy efficiency and comfort at home, while helping to protect the environment.<sup id="cite_ref-energystar.gov_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-energystar.gov-5"><span>[</span>5<span>]</span></a></sup> The Home Performance with ENERGY STAR program has 40 programs, to date, across the country which are administered by various utilities and state energy offices.<sup id="cite_ref-HpwES_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HpwES-6"><span>[</span>6<span>]</span></a></sup></p> 
     <p>Home performance applies <a href="/wiki/Building_science" title="Building science">building science</a> to address the following issues:<sup id="cite_ref-STLCC.edu_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-STLCC.edu-7"><span>[</span>7<span>]</span></a></sup></p> 
     <ul> 
      <li><a href="/wiki/Efficient_energy_use" title="Efficient energy use">Efficient energy use</a></li> 
      <li><a href="//en.wiktionary.org/wiki/durability" class="extiw" title="wikt:durability">Durability</a></li> 
      <li><a href="/wiki/Health_%26_Safety" title="Health &amp; Safety" class="mw-redirect">Health &amp; Safety</a></li> 
      <li><a href="/wiki/Indoor_Air_Quality" title="Indoor Air Quality" class="mw-redirect">Indoor Air Quality</a><sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8"><span>[</span>8<span>]</span></a></sup></li> 
      <li><a href="/wiki/Thermal_Comfort" title="Thermal Comfort" class="mw-redirect">Thermal Comfort</a></li> 
      <li>Indoor Moisture sources and solutions<sup id="cite_ref-CSUohio_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CSUohio-9"><span>[</span>9<span>]</span></a></sup></li> 
     </ul> 
     <p>Diagnostic equipment includes:<sup id="cite_ref-STLCC.edu_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-STLCC.edu-7"><span>[</span>7<span>]</span></a></sup></p> 
     <ul> 
      <li><a href="/wiki/Blower_door" title="Blower door">Blower door</a></li> 
      <li><a href="/w/index.php?title=Duct_leakage_tester_(Duct_Blaster_(R))&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Duct leakage tester (Duct Blaster (R)) (page does not exist)">Duct leakage tester (Duct Blaster (R))</a></li> 
      <li><a href="/wiki/Thermal_Imaging_camera" title="Thermal Imaging camera" class="mw-redirect">Thermal Imaging camera</a></li> 
      <li><a href="/w/index.php?title=Combustion_analyzer&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Combustion analyzer (page does not exist)">Combustion analyzer</a></li> 
      <li><a href="/wiki/Carbon_monoxide_detector" title="Carbon monoxide detector">Carbon monoxide detector</a></li> 
     </ul> 
     <p>A home performance energy audit results in <a href="/wiki/Home_energy_retrofit" title="Home energy retrofit" class="mw-redirect">home energy retrofit</a> recommendations to improve the comfort and efficiency of the home, the most common of which include:</p> 
     <ul> 
      <li>Sealing air holes and gaps in the <a href="/wiki/Building_envelope" title="Building envelope">building envelope</a></li> 
      <li>Sealing &amp; insulating structures like attic access stairways &amp; whole house fans</li> 
      <li>installing new windows or sealing existing windows</li> 
      <li>Adding insulation.</li> 
      <li>Sealing <a href="/wiki/Duct_(HVAC)" title="Duct (HVAC)" class="mw-redirect">ducts</a>.</li> 
      <li>Upgrading <a href="/wiki/HVAC" title="HVAC">heating and cooling systems</a>, <a href="/wiki/Home_appliance" title="Home appliance">appliances</a> and <a href="/wiki/Lighting" title="Lighting">lighting</a> by replacing them with energy efficient equipment such as <a href="/wiki/Energy_star" title="Energy star" class="mw-redirect">ENERGY STAR</a> appliances or devices, <a href="/wiki/Compact_fluorescent_lamp" title="Compact fluorescent lamp">compact fluorescent lamps</a>, or <a href="/wiki/LED_lamp" title="LED lamp">LED</a> lamps.</li> 
      <li>Adding <a href="/wiki/Heat_recovery_ventilation" title="Heat recovery ventilation">heat recovery ventilation</a> or other means to control and upgrade fresh air ventilation.</li> 
      <li>Controlling moisture.</li> 
     </ul>
