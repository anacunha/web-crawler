     <p><b>North Kivu</b> (<b>Nord-Kivu</b> in <a href="/wiki/French_language" title="French language">French</a>) is a <a href="/wiki/Provinces_of_the_Democratic_Republic_of_the_Congo" title="Provinces of the Democratic Republic of the Congo">province</a> bordering <a href="/wiki/Lake_Kivu" title="Lake Kivu">Lake Kivu</a> in the eastern <a href="/wiki/Democratic_Republic_of_the_Congo" title="Democratic Republic of the Congo">Democratic Republic of the Congo</a>. Its <a href="/wiki/Capital_city" title="Capital city">capital</a> is <a href="/wiki/Goma" title="Goma">Goma</a>.</p> 
     <p>North Kivu borders the provinces of <a href="/wiki/Orientale_Province" title="Orientale Province">Orientale</a> to the north and northwest, <a href="/wiki/Maniema" title="Maniema">Maniema</a> to the southwest, and <a href="/wiki/South_Kivu" title="South Kivu">South Kivu</a> to the south. To the east it borders the countries of <a href="/wiki/Uganda" title="Uganda">Uganda</a> and <a href="/wiki/Rwanda" title="Rwanda">Rwanda</a>.</p> 
     <p>The province consists of three cities—<a href="/wiki/Goma" title="Goma">Goma</a>, <a href="/wiki/Butembo" title="Butembo">Butembo</a> and <a href="/wiki/Beni,_Democratic_Republic_of_the_Congo" title="Beni, Democratic Republic of the Congo">Beni</a>—and six <a href="/wiki/Territories_of_the_Democratic_Republic_of_the_Congo" title="Territories of the Democratic Republic of the Congo">territories</a>—<a href="/wiki/Beni_Territory" title="Beni Territory">Beni</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lubero_Territory" title="Lubero Territory">Lubero</a>, <a href="/wiki/Masisi_Territory" title="Masisi Territory">Masisi</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rutshuru_Territory" title="Rutshuru Territory">Rutshuru</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nyiragongo_Territory" title="Nyiragongo Territory">Nyiragongo</a> and <a href="/wiki/Walikale_Territory" title="Walikale Territory">Walikale</a>. The province is home to the <a href="/wiki/Virunga_National_Park" title="Virunga National Park">Virunga National Park</a>, a <a href="/wiki/World_Heritage_Site" title="World Heritage Site">World Heritage Site</a> containing the endangered <a href="/wiki/Mountain_gorilla" title="Mountain gorilla">mountain gorillas</a>.</p> 
     <p>The region is politically unstable, and has been one of the flashpoints in military conflicts in the region since 1998.</p>

     <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=North_Kivu&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
     <p>North Kivu was formerly a "sub-region" in the region of <a href="/wiki/Kivu" title="Kivu">Kivu</a>.</p> 
     <p>The region was the scene of much fighting during the <a href="/wiki/Second_Congo_War" title="Second Congo War">Second Congo War</a> (1998–2003), and the <a href="/wiki/Kivu_conflict" title="Kivu conflict">Kivu conflict</a> (2004–present).</p> 
     <p><a href="/wiki/Laurent_Nkunda" title="Laurent Nkunda">Laurent Nkunda</a> was offered the rank of Brigadier General and command of the new Congo Government's <a href="/wiki/Military_of_the_Democratic_Republic_of_the_Congo" title="Military of the Democratic Republic of the Congo" class="mw-redirect">FARDC</a> Eighth Military Region, covering North Kivu, by DRC government decree 019/2003 of 19 August 2003. However, he refused to take up the post. On 26 May 2004, General Obed Wibasira (RCD-Goma) was named to the position. However, Wibasira was suspected of complicity with the soldiers in Goma who triggered a mutiny in Bukavu in February 2004, and on 23 January 2005 he was switched with <a href="/wiki/Gabriel_Amisi_Kumba" title="Gabriel Amisi Kumba">Gabriel Amisi Kumba</a>, at the time commander of the Fifth Military Region in <a href="/wiki/Kasa%C3%AF-Oriental" title="Kasaï-Oriental" class="mw-redirect">Kasaï-Oriental</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> Gabriel Amisi Kumba was named as a Brigadier General when taking up the post. General Louis Ngizo, a former commander of the <a href="/wiki/Rally_for_Congolese_Democracy" title="Rally for Congolese Democracy">Rally for Congolese Democracy</a>, was appointed commander in November 2006. However he was of little influence compared to powerful military figures from Kinshasa, U.S. diplomats said in comments released via <a href="/wiki/WikiLeaks" title="WikiLeaks">WikiLeaks</a>. Brigadier General Vainqueur Mayala was transferred from command of the Ituri operational zone, promoted to Major General, and appointed military region commander in May 2007.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> Ngizo left Goma on 13 May 2007, his next posting not being known at the time. During late 2008, the FARDC maintained its dismal record in combat against <a href="/wiki/Laurent_Nkunda" title="Laurent Nkunda">Laurent Nkunda</a>'s CNDP faction, losing the <a href="/wiki/Rumangabo" title="Rumangabo">Rumangabo</a> military camp to the rebels.</p> 
     <p>The dissident <a href="/wiki/Mai-Mai" title="Mai-Mai">Mai-Mai</a> 85th Brigade, commanded by Colonel Samy Matumo, controlled the <a href="/wiki/Cassiterite" title="Cassiterite">cassiterite</a> mine at <a href="/wiki/Bisie" title="Bisie">Bisie</a>, just north of Manoire in <a href="/wiki/Walikale" title="Walikale">Walikale</a>, in the south-east of North Kivu.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> The former <a href="/wiki/Rally_for_Congolese_Democracy" title="Rally for Congolese Democracy">RCD-K/ML</a> also has fighters in the province; 'at the beginning of the transition [2002-3] the RCD-Mouvement de Libération president declared he had 8-10,000 Armée Patriotic Congolaise (APC) troops in the Beni-Lubero area of North Kivu.’ This exaggerated figure now seems to have been reduced to ‘several thousand’ (2-4,000?) as of early 2006 following demobilizations and men joining the integrated brigades.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>

     <p>In October 2007, the <a href="/wiki/United_Nations_High_Commissioner_for_Refugees" title="United Nations High Commissioner for Refugees">United Nations High Commissioner for Refugees</a> (UNHCR) warned of an increasing number of <a href="/wiki/Internally_displaced_person" title="Internally displaced person">internally displaced people</a> (IDP) in North Kivu related to the <a href="/wiki/Kivu_conflict" title="Kivu conflict">fighting</a> there between the government <a href="/wiki/Military_of_the_Democratic_Republic_of_the_Congo" title="Military of the Democratic Republic of the Congo" class="mw-redirect">army</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Democratic_Forces_for_the_Liberation_of_Rwanda" title="Democratic Forces for the Liberation of Rwanda">Democratic Forces for the Liberation of Rwanda</a> (FDLR) rebels and renegade troops, including <a href="/wiki/Laurent_Nkunda" title="Laurent Nkunda">Laurent Nkunda</a>'s forces, and a build-up of military supplies and forces, including the reported recruitment of <a href="/wiki/Military_use_of_children" title="Military use of children">child soldiers</a> by armed groups across North Kivu. The UNHCR thought that there were over 370,000 people in North Kivu displaced since December 2006, and is expanding its camps in the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Mugunga&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Mugunga (page does not exist)">Mugunga</a> area where over 80,000 IDPs were estimated.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> The brief capture of Goma, by M23 rebels caused "tens of thousands" of refugees. The town of Sake was abandoned.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
