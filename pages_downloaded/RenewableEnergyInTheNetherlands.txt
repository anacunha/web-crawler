   <h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">Renewable energy in the Netherlands</h1> 
   <div id="bodyContent" class="mw-body-content"> 
    <div id="siteSub">
     From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia
    </div> 
    <div id="contentSub"></div> 
    <div id="jump-to-nav" class="mw-jump">
      Jump to: 
     <a href="#mw-head">navigation</a>, 
     <a href="#p-search">search</a> 
    </div> 
    <div id="mw-content-text" lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr">
     <p>Despite the historic usage of wind power to drain water and grind grain, the <a href="/wiki/Netherlands" title="Netherlands">Netherlands</a> lags behind most <a href="/wiki/European_Union" title="European Union">EU</a> countries in the production of energy from <a href="/wiki/Renewable_energy" title="Renewable energy">renewable sources</a>. The flat and often sub-sea level landscape limits <a href="/wiki/Hydropower" title="Hydropower">hydropower</a> resources and the country does not lie in a region of high <a href="/wiki/Geothermal" title="Geothermal" class="mw-redirect">geothermal</a> potential. The leading renewable power sources are <a href="/wiki/Biomass" title="Biomass">biomass</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wind_power" title="Wind power">wind</a>, <a href="/wiki/Solar_power" title="Solar power">solar</a> and geothermal. In 2010, the Netherlands produced only 3.7%, up from only 1% in 1990.<sup id="cite_ref-Stat2010_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Stat2010-1"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup></p> 
     <p>Three hydropower plants provide almost all of the Netherlands' hydropower. In 2010, the Netherlands had 1973 wind turbines including 98 in two offshore windfarms. The turbines had a total nameplate capacity of 2237 MW. Flevoland was the leading province for wind energy with Groningen second in capacity and production. The Netherlands had 88 MW of solar electricity and 98 MW of manure digesters in 2010.<sup id="cite_ref-Stat2010_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Stat2010-1"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup></p> 
     <p>In 2009 the Netherlands used 3,9% wind power of electricity (278/ 7,073) <sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup> The wind capacity installed at end 2010 will, in a normal wind year, produce 4.1% of electricity, when the equivalent value for <a href="/wiki/Wind_power_in_Germany" title="Wind power in Germany">Germany</a> is 9.4% and <a href="/wiki/Wind_power_in_Portugal" title="Wind power in Portugal">Portugal</a> 14%.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup></p> 
     <p>In the <a href="/wiki/Netherlands" title="Netherlands">Netherlands</a>, household consumers can choose to buy <a href="/wiki/Renewable_energy" title="Renewable energy">renewable electricity</a>. For 2008, the amount of renewable energy used by household users is increasing. Halfway through 2010 it was 44%, up from 38% in 2008 and 41% in 2009.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup> A large part of the renewable electricity sold in the Netherlands comes from <a href="/wiki/Norway" title="Norway">Norway</a>, a country which generates <a href="/wiki/Electricity_sector_in_Norway#Fuel_mix_disclosure" title="Electricity sector in Norway">almost all its electricity</a> with <a href="/wiki/Hydroelectricity" title="Hydroelectricity">hydropower plants</a>.</p>

     <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Climate_change">Climate change</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Renewable_energy_in_the_Netherlands&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Climate change">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
     <p>According to Prime Minister <a href="/wiki/Jan_Peter_Balkenende" title="Jan Peter Balkenende">Jan Peter Balkenende</a>, the Netherlands use annually 1-1.5 billion € (0.3% of national income) to protect against the risks of the sea level rise. Many areas are under the sea level in the Netherlands and are protected by dam and dikes.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5"><span>[</span>5<span>]</span></a></sup> The Netherlands supported in 2010 rising the European Union emission restrictions from 20% to 30%; however, the Netherlands has only committed to reaching the minimum 14% goal for itself.<sup id="cite_ref-Stat2010_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Stat2010-1"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
