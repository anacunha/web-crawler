     <p>The <b>Svartsengi Power Station</b> (<i>Svartsengi</i> (<small>Icelandic pronunciation:&nbsp;</small><span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_Icelandic" title="Help:IPA for Icelandic">[ÀàsvarÃ•sÀå…õiÃØ…≤c…™]</a></span>); "black meadow" in <a href="/wiki/Icelandic_language" title="Icelandic language">Icelandic</a>) is a <a href="/wiki/Geothermal_power" title="Geothermal power" class="mw-redirect">geothermal</a> power station located about 4&nbsp;km north of <a href="/wiki/Grindav%C3%ADk" title="Grindav√≠k">Grindav√≠k</a>, <a href="/wiki/Iceland" title="Iceland">Iceland</a>, near the <a href="/wiki/Keflav%C3%ADk_International_Airport" title="Keflav√≠k International Airport">Keflav√≠k International Airport</a> on the <a href="/wiki/Reykjanes" title="Reykjanes">Reykjanes Peninsula</a>. As of December 2007, it produces 76.5 <a href="/wiki/Megawatt" title="Megawatt" class="mw-redirect">MW</a> of energy, and about 475 litres/second of 90&nbsp;¬∞C (194&nbsp;¬∞F) hot water (ca. 80 MWt). Surplus mineral-rich water from the plant fills up the <a href="/wiki/Blue_Lagoon_(geothermal_spa)" title="Blue Lagoon (geothermal spa)">Blue Lagoon</a>, a popular bathing resort.</p> 
     <p><a href="/wiki/Carbon_Recycling_International" title="Carbon Recycling International">Carbon Recycling International</a>'s first renewable methanol plant is located next to Svartsengi.</p>
