     <p><br> The <b>Whole Building Design Guide</b> or WBDG is described by the <a href="/wiki/Federal_Energy_Management_Program" title="Federal Energy Management Program">Federal Energy Management Program</a> as "a complete internet resource to a wide range of building-related design guidance, criteria and technology", and meets the requirements in guidance documents for <a href="/wiki/Executive_Order" title="Executive Order" class="mw-redirect">Executive Order</a> 13123.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup> WBDG is based on the premise that to create a successful high-performance building, one must apply an integrated design and team approach in all phases of a project, including planning, design, construction, operations and maintenance. The WBDG is managed by the <a href="/wiki/National_Institute_of_Building_Sciences" title="National Institute of Building Sciences">National Institute of Building Sciences</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-NIBSFactSheet_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NIBSFactSheet-2"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup></p>

     <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Whole_Building_Design_Guide&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
     <p>The WBDG was initially designed to serve <a href="/wiki/U.S._Department_of_Defense" title="U.S. Department of Defense" class="mw-redirect">U.S. Department of Defense</a> (DOD) construction programs. A 2003 DOD memorandum named WBDG the “sole portal to design and construction criteria produced by the <a href="/wiki/U.S._Army_Corps_of_Engineers" title="U.S. Army Corps of Engineers" class="mw-redirect">U.S. Army Corps of Engineers</a> (USACE), <a href="/wiki/Naval_Facilities_Engineering_Command" title="Naval Facilities Engineering Command">Naval Facilities Engineering Command</a> (NAVFAC), and <a href="/wiki/U.S._Air_Force" title="U.S. Air Force" class="mw-redirect">U.S. Air Force</a>.” Since then, WBDG has expanded to serve all building industry professionals. The majority of users are from the private sector.<sup id="cite_ref-NIBSFactSheet_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NIBSFactSheet-2"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup> The WBDG draws information from the Construction Criteria Base and a privately owned database run by Information Handling Services.<sup id="cite_ref-NIBSFactSheet_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NIBSFactSheet-2"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup></p> 
     <p>A significant amount of the Whole Building Design Guide content is organized by three categories: Design Guidance, Project Management, and Operations and Maintenance. It is structured to provide WBDG visitors first a broad understanding then increasingly specific information more targeted towards building industry professionals. WBDG is the resource that federal agencies look to for policy and technical guidance on Federal High Performance and Sustainable Buildings<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup> In addition, the WBDG contains online tools,<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup> the original Construction Criteria Base,<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5"><span>[</span>5<span>]</span></a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Building_Information_Modeling" title="Building Information Modeling" class="mw-redirect">Building Information Modeling</a> guides and libraries, a database of select case studies, federal mandates and other resources. The WBDG also provides online continuing education courses for architects and other building professionals, free of charge.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6"><span>[</span>6<span>]</span></a></sup></p> 
     <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Development">Development</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Whole_Building_Design_Guide&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Development">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
     <p>Development of the WBDG is a collaborative effort among federal agencies, private sector companies, non-profit organizations and educational institutions. The WBDG web site maintained by the <a href="/wiki/National_Institute_of_Building_Sciences" title="National Institute of Building Sciences">National Institute of Building Sciences</a> through funding support from the <a href="/wiki/U.S._Department_of_Defense" title="U.S. Department of Defense" class="mw-redirect">DOD</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Naval_Facilities_Engineering_Command" title="Naval Facilities Engineering Command">NAVFAC</a> Engineering Innovation and Criteria Office, <a href="/wiki/USACE" title="USACE" class="mw-redirect">U.S. Army Corps of Engineers</a>, the <a href="/wiki/U.S._Air_Force" title="U.S. Air Force" class="mw-redirect">U.S. Air Force</a>, the <a href="/wiki/U.S._General_Services_Administration" title="U.S. General Services Administration" class="mw-redirect">U.S. General Services Administration</a> (GSA), the <a href="/wiki/U.S._Department_of_Veterans_Affairs" title="U.S. Department of Veterans Affairs" class="mw-redirect">U.S. Department of Veterans Affairs</a>, the <a href="/wiki/National_Aeronautics_and_Space_Administration" title="National Aeronautics and Space Administration" class="mw-redirect">National Aeronautics and Space Administration</a> (NASA), and the <a href="/wiki/U.S._Department_of_Energy" title="U.S. Department of Energy" class="mw-redirect">U.S. Department of Energy</a> (DOE), and the assistance of the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Sustainable_Buildings_Industry_Council&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Sustainable Buildings Industry Council (page does not exist)">Sustainable Buildings Industry Council</a> (SBIC). A Board of Direction and an Advisory Committee consisting of representatives from over 25 participating federal agencies guide the development of the WBDG.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7"><span>[</span>7<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
