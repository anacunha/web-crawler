     <p>The <b>University of Zagreb</b> (<a href="/wiki/Croatian_language" title="Croatian language">Croatian</a>: <span lang="hr" xml:lang="hr"><i>Sveučilište u Zagrebu</i></span>, <small>pronounced&nbsp;</small><span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_Serbo-Croatian" title="Help:IPA for Serbo-Croatian">[sʋeǔt͡ʃiliːʃte u zǎːgrebu]</a></span>; <a href="/wiki/Latin_language" title="Latin language" class="mw-redirect">Latin</a>: <span lang="la" xml:lang="la"><i>Universitas Studiorum Zagrabiensis</i></span>) is the largest <a href="/wiki/Croatia" title="Croatia">Croatian</a> <a href="/wiki/University" title="University">university</a> and the oldest continuously operating university in the area covering Central Europe south of <a href="/wiki/Vienna" title="Vienna">Vienna</a> and all of <a href="/wiki/Southeastern_Europe" title="Southeastern Europe" class="mw-redirect">Southeastern Europe</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p> 
     <p>History of the University began on September 23, 1669 when the Holy Roman Emperor <a href="/wiki/Leopold_I,_Holy_Roman_Emperor" title="Leopold I, Holy Roman Emperor">Leopold I</a> issued a decree granting the establishment of the <i>Jesuit Academy of the Royal Free City of Zagreb</i>. Decree was accepted at the Council of the Croatian Kingdom on November 3, 1671. The Academy was run by the <a href="/wiki/Jesuits" title="Jesuits" class="mw-redirect">Jesuits</a> for more than a century until the order was dissolved by <a href="/wiki/Pope_Clement_XIV" title="Pope Clement XIV">Pope Clement XIV</a> in 1773. In 1776 Empress <a href="/wiki/Maria_Theresa" title="Maria Theresa">Maria Theresa</a> issued a decree founding the <i>Royal Academy of Science</i> which succeeded the previous Jesuit Academy. Bishop <a href="/wiki/Josip_Juraj_Strossmayer" title="Josip Juraj Strossmayer">Josip Juraj Strossmayer</a> proposed to the <a href="/wiki/Croatian_Parliament" title="Croatian Parliament">Croatian Parliament</a> in 1861 founding of a University. Emperor <a href="/wiki/Franz_Joseph" title="Franz Joseph" class="mw-redirect">Franz Joseph</a> signed the decree on the establishment of the University of Zagreb in 1869. Act of Founding was passed by the Parliament in 1874, and was ratified by the Emperor on January 5, 1874. On October 19, 1874, a <i>Royal University of Franz Joseph I</i> was official opened.</p> 
     <p>University comprises 29 faculties, 3 art academies and 1 university center with more than 70.000 students. University is as of 2015 at a 551 place out of 1000 on the list of Universities of the world made by the Center for University World Rankings.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>

     <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Academy">Academy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=University_of_Zagreb&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Academy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
     <p>The beginnings of the later university date back to 23 September 1669 when Emperor and King <a href="/wiki/Leopold_I,_Holy_Roman_Emperor" title="Leopold I, Holy Roman Emperor">Leopold I</a> Habsburg issued a decree granting the establishment of the <a href="/wiki/Jesuit" title="Jesuit" class="mw-redirect">Jesuit</a> Academy of the Royal Free City of <a href="/wiki/Zagreb" title="Zagreb">Zagreb</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-R.C3.BCegg_685_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-R.C3.BCegg_685-4">[4]</a></sup> According to that document the study of philosophy in Zagreb acquired a formal and legal status as <i>Neoacademia Zagrabiensis</i> and officially became a public institution of higher education.</p> 
     <p>The academy was run by the Jesuits for more than a century until the order was dissolved by Pope <a href="/wiki/Clement_XIV" title="Clement XIV" class="mw-redirect">Clement XIV</a> in 1773. Under a new leadership in 1772 the academy enrolled a total of 200 students.</p> 
     <p>In 1776 Empress and Queen <a href="/wiki/Maria_Theresa_of_Austria" title="Maria Theresa of Austria" class="mw-redirect">Maria Theresa</a> issued a decree founding the Royal Academy of Science (<a href="/wiki/Latin_language" title="Latin language" class="mw-redirect">Latin</a>: <span lang="la" xml:lang="la"><i>Regia Scientiarum Academia</i></span>).<sup id="cite_ref-R.C3.BCegg_685_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-R.C3.BCegg_685-4">[4]</a></sup> It consisted of three studies or <a href="/wiki/Faculty_(division)" title="Faculty (division)">faculties</a> of philosophy, theology, and law. The former political-<a href="/wiki/Cameral" title="Cameral" class="mw-redirect">cameral</a> studies became part of the newly established faculty of law, and thus were integrated into the academy. Each of the faculties of the Royal Academy of Sciences had several chairs teaching one or several courses.</p> 
     <p>The academy in Zagreb remained until 1874, despite numerous organizational changes, the focal institution of higher education in Croatia, educating most of the members of the Croatian intelligentsia.</p> 
     <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="University">University</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=University_of_Zagreb&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: University">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
     <div role="note" class="hatnote">
      See also: 
      <a href="/wiki/Franz_Joseph_University_(disambiguation)" title="Franz Joseph University (disambiguation)" class="mw-disambig">Franz Joseph University (disambiguation)</a>
     </div> 
     <p>Bishop <a href="/wiki/Josip_Juraj_Strossmayer" title="Josip Juraj Strossmayer">Josip Juraj Strossmayer</a> in 1861 proposed to the <a href="/wiki/Croatian_Parliament" title="Croatian Parliament">Croatian Parliament</a> the founding of a university at Zagreb. During his visit in 1869, the Emperor <a href="/wiki/Franz_Joseph" title="Franz Joseph" class="mw-redirect">Franz Joseph</a> signed the decree on the establishment of the University of Zagreb. Five years later, the Parliament passed the Act of Founding, which was ratified by the Emperor on 5 January 1874. On 19 October 1874, a ceremony was held in the name of the founding of the <i>Royal University of Franz Joseph I in Zagreb</i>,<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> making it the third university in the <a href="/wiki/Transleithania" title="Transleithania" class="mw-redirect">Hungarian realm</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Austro-Hungarian_Empire" title="Austro-Hungarian Empire" class="mw-redirect">Austro-Hungarian Empire</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p> 
     <p>In 1874 the University had four faculties:</p> 
     <ul> 
      <li>Law (<i>Pravno-državoslovni fakultet</i>)</li> 
      <li>Theology (<i>Bogoslovni fakultet</i>)</li> 
      <li>Philosophy (<i>Mudroslovni fakultet</i>)</li> 
      <li>Medicine (<i>Liječnički fakultet</i>)</li> 
     </ul> 
     <p>The Faculty of Medicine was not put into function in 1874; it had to wait until 1917. The Faculty of Philosophy served as the general scientific faculty. Since 1876 it had geology, botany, physics, mathematics, and chemistry; since 1877 zoology; since 1882 pharmacy; since 1883 geography.</p> 
     <p>In 1860, the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Royal_Agriculture_and_Forestry_College&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Royal Agriculture and Forestry College (page does not exist)">Royal Agriculture and Forestry College</a> was founded in <a href="/wiki/Kri%C5%BEevci,_Croatia" title="Križevci, Croatia">Križevci</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> In 1898, the Academy of Forestry (<i>Šumarska akademija</i>) was founded as part of the Faculty of Philosophy, which encompassed all technical studies. In 1919, this school became the Faculty of Husbandry and Forestry.</p> 
     <p>In 1919, the School of Technology (<i>Tehnička visoka škola</i>) was founded, which was transformed into a university faculty in 1926. Also in 1919 the School of Veterinary Medicine (<i>Veterinarska visoka škola</i>) was founded; it transformed into a university faculty in 1925.</p> 
     <p>In the Faculty of Philosophy, major reorganization ensued in the 1920s, as mathematics, pharmacy and other sciences started to split off, first with the creation of separate mathematics and pharmaceutical departments in 1928, when the faculty was renamed into its current name <i>Filozofski fakultet</i>.</p> 
     <p>In 1926, the university was composed of seven faculties:</p> 
     <ul> 
      <li>Theology (<i>Bogoslovni fakultet</i>)</li> 
      <li>Law (<i>Pravnički fakultet</i>)</li> 
      <li>Medicine (<i>Liječnički fakultet</i>)</li> 
      <li>Philosophy (<i>Mudroslovni fakultet</i>) 
       <ul> 
        <li>Philosophy dept. (<i>Filozofski odjel</i>)</li> 
        <li>Pharmacy dept. (<i>Farmaceutski odjel</i>)</li> 
       </ul> </li> 
      <li>Husbandry and Forestry (<i>Gospodarsko-šumarski fakultet</i>)</li> 
      <li>Veterinary Medicine (<i>Veterinarski fakultet</i>)</li> 
      <li>Technology (<i>Tehnički fakultet</i>) 
       <ul> 
        <li>Construction dept. (<i>Građevni odsjek</i>)</li> 
        <li>Engineering dept. (<i>Strojarski odsjek</i>)</li> 
        <li>Chemical engineering dept. (<i>Kemijsko-inženjerski odsjek</i>)</li> 
       </ul> </li> 
     </ul> 
     <p>During the <a href="/wiki/Independent_State_of_Croatia" title="Independent State of Croatia">Independent State of Croatia</a> (1941–1945), the university was known as the <i>Croatian University</i> (Hrvatsko sveučilište).</p> 
     <p>The individual departments of the Faculty of Philosophy became separate faculties in 1942, 1946 when the Faculty of Sciences was formed, and finally in 1963.</p> 
     <p>In 1956, the Faculty of Technology was divided into four faculties:</p> 
     <ul> 
      <li>Architecture-Construction-Geodesy (<i>Arhitektonsko-građevinsko-geodetski fakultet</i>)</li> 
      <li>Electrical engineering (<i>Elektrotehnički fakultet</i>)</li> 
      <li>Mechanical engineering-Shipbuilding (<i>Strojarsko-brodograđevni fakultet</i>)</li> 
      <li>Chemistry-Food technology-Mining (<i>Tehnološki fakultet</i>)</li> 
     </ul> 
     <p>These eventually split up into the current layout.</p> 
     <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Discrimination_affair">Discrimination affair</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=University_of_Zagreb&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Discrimination affair">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
     <p>Dario Kresic - a senior research assistant at the University of Zagreb, <a href="/wiki/Faculty_of_Organization_and_Informatics,_University_of_Zagreb" title="Faculty of Organization and Informatics, University of Zagreb" class="mw-redirect">Faculty of Organization and Informatics</a> - brought in 2010 a lawsuit against the <a href="/wiki/Faculty_of_Organization_and_Informatics,_University_of_Zagreb" title="Faculty of Organization and Informatics, University of Zagreb" class="mw-redirect">Faculty of Organization and Informatics</a> claiming he was discriminated on the grounds of his sexual orientation.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> In 2009, there was a history of harassment based on sexual orientation against Kresic by his colleagues at the <a href="/wiki/Faculty_of_Organization_and_Informatics,_University_of_Zagreb" title="Faculty of Organization and Informatics, University of Zagreb" class="mw-redirect">Faculty of Organization and Informatics</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> During 2010 Kresic faced discrimination in promotion at the workplace; after he brought a complaint to the Dean of the <a href="/wiki/Faculty_of_Organization_and_Informatics,_University_of_Zagreb" title="Faculty of Organization and Informatics, University of Zagreb" class="mw-redirect">Faculty of Organization and Informatics</a> about the harassment, the procedure regarding his application for the position of an assistant professor was obstructed by the <a href="/wiki/Faculty_of_Organization_and_Informatics,_University_of_Zagreb" title="Faculty of Organization and Informatics, University of Zagreb" class="mw-redirect">Faculty of Organization and Informatics</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p> 
     <p>In July 2012 the Municipal Court in <a href="/wiki/Vara%C5%BEdin" title="Varaždin">Varaždin</a> found that the <a href="/wiki/Faculty_of_Organization_and_Informatics,_University_of_Zagreb" title="Faculty of Organization and Informatics, University of Zagreb" class="mw-redirect">Faculty of Organization and Informatics</a> discriminated and harassed Kresic on the grounds of sexual orientation and the Court ordered the <a href="/wiki/Faculty_of_Organization_and_Informatics,_University_of_Zagreb" title="Faculty of Organization and Informatics, University of Zagreb" class="mw-redirect">Faculty of Organization and Informatics</a> to refrain from any actions that would prevent Kresic from career and academic advancement.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> This case was the first lawsuit of its kind in <a href="/wiki/Croatia" title="Croatia">Croatia</a>.</p>
