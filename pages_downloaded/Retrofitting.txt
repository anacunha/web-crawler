     <p><b>Retrofitting</b> refers to the addition of new technology or features to older systems.</p> 
     <ul> 
      <li><a href="/wiki/Power_plant" title="Power plant" class="mw-redirect">power plant</a> retrofit, improving power plant efficiency / increasing output / reducing emissions</li> 
      <li><a href="/wiki/Home_energy_retrofit" title="Home energy retrofit" class="mw-redirect">home energy retrofit</a>, the improving of existing buildings with energy efficiency equipment</li> 
      <li><a href="/wiki/Seismic_retrofit" title="Seismic retrofit">seismic retrofit</a>, the process of strengthening older buildings in order to make them earthquake resistant</li> 
     </ul>

     <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Benefits_of_a_retrofit">Benefits of a retrofit</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Retrofitting&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Benefits of a retrofit">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
     <ul> 
      <li>Saving on <a href="/wiki/Capex" title="Capex" class="mw-redirect">capex</a> while benefiting from new technologies</li> 
      <li>Optimization of existing plant components</li> 
      <li>Adaptation of the plant for new or changed products</li> 
      <li>Increase in piece number and cycle time</li> 
      <li>Guaranteed spare parts availability<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup></li>

     <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Manufacturing">Manufacturing</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Retrofitting&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Manufacturing">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
     <p>Principally retrofitting describes the measures taken in the manufacturing industry to allow new or updated parts to be fitted to old or outdated assemblies (like blades to wind turbines).</p> 
     <p>The production of retrofit parts is necessary in manufacture when the design of a large assembly is changed or revised. If, after the changes have been implemented, a customer (with an old version of the product) wishes to purchase a replacement part then retrofit parts and assembling techniques will have to be used so that the revised parts will fit suitably onto the older assembly.</p> 
     <p>Another example of this is <a href="/wiki/Custom_car" title="Custom car">car customizing</a>, where older vehicles are fitted with new technologies: <a href="/wiki/Power_window" title="Power window">power windows</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cruise_control" title="Cruise control">cruise control</a>, <a href="/wiki/Remote_keyless_system" title="Remote keyless system">remote keyless systems</a>, electric <a href="/wiki/Fuel_pump" title="Fuel pump">fuel pumps</a>, etc.</p> 
     <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Environmental_management">Environmental management</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Retrofitting&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Environmental management">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
     <p>The term is also used in the field of <a href="/wiki/Environmental_engineering" title="Environmental engineering">environmental engineering</a>, particularly to describe <a href="/wiki/Construction" title="Construction">construction</a> or renovation projects on previously built sites, to improve <a href="/wiki/Water_quality" title="Water quality">water quality</a> in nearby <a href="/wiki/Stream" title="Stream">streams</a>, <a href="/wiki/River" title="River">rivers</a> or <a href="/wiki/Lake" title="Lake">lakes</a>. The concept has also been applied to changing the output mix of <a href="/wiki/Energy" title="Energy">energy</a> from <a href="/wiki/Power_plant" title="Power plant" class="mw-redirect">power plants</a> to <a href="/wiki/Cogeneration" title="Cogeneration">cogeneration</a> in <a href="/wiki/Urban_area" title="Urban area">urban areas</a> with a potential for <a href="/wiki/District_heating" title="District heating">district heating</a>.</p> 
     <p>Sites with extensive <a href="/wiki/Impervious_surface" title="Impervious surface">impervious surfaces</a> (such as parking lots and rooftops) can generate high levels of <a href="/wiki/Stormwater" title="Stormwater">stormwater</a> <a href="/wiki/Surface_runoff" title="Surface runoff">runoff</a> during rainstorms, and this can damage nearby water bodies. These problems can often be addressed by installing new stormwater management features on the site, a process that practitioners refer to as stormwater retrofitting. Stormwater management practices used in retrofit projects include <a href="/wiki/Rain_garden" title="Rain garden">rain gardens</a>, <a href="/wiki/Permeable_paving" title="Permeable paving">permeable paving</a> and <a href="/wiki/Green_roof" title="Green roof">green roofs</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup> <i>(See also <a href="/wiki/Stream_restoration" title="Stream restoration">stream restoration</a>.)</i></p>
