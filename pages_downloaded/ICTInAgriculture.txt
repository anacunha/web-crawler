     <p>The application of <b>information and communications technology (ICT) in agriculture</b> is increasingly important.</p> 
     <p>E-Agriculture is an emerging field focusing on the enhancement of agricultural and rural development through improved information and communication processes. More specifically, e-Agriculture involves the conceptualization, design, development, evaluation and application of innovative ways to use information and communication technologies (IT) in the rural domain, with a primary focus on agriculture. E-Agriculture is a relatively new term and we fully expect its scope to change and evolve as our understanding of the area grows.Indian Agriculture contributes to 18.6 per cent of India’s GDP, and approximately 59 per cent Indians derive their livelihood from the agricultural sector. Private sector initiatives like contract farming have commercialized the Indian agricultural sector.</p> 
     <p>E-Agriculture is one of the action lines identified in the declaration and plan of action of the <a href="/wiki/World_Summit_on_the_Information_Society" title="World Summit on the Information Society">World Summit on the Information Society</a> (WSIS). The "Tunis Agenda for the Information Society," published on 18 November 2005 and emphasizes the leading facilitating roles that UN agencies need to play in the implementation of the Geneva Plan of Action. The Food and Agriculture Organization of the United Nations (<a href="/wiki/FAO" title="FAO" class="mw-redirect">FAO</a>) has been assigned the responsibility of organizing activities related to the action line under C.7 ICT Applications on E-Agriculture.</p> 
     <p>The main phases of the <a href="/wiki/Agriculture" title="Agriculture">agriculture</a> industry include <a href="/wiki/Plant_cultivation" title="Plant cultivation" class="mw-redirect">crop cultivation</a>, <a href="/wiki/Water_management" title="Water management" class="mw-redirect">water management</a>, <a href="/wiki/Fertilizer" title="Fertilizer">fertilizer</a> application, <a href="/wiki/Fertigation" title="Fertigation">fertigation</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pest_control" title="Pest control">pest management</a>, <a href="/wiki/Harvesting" title="Harvesting" class="mw-redirect">harvesting</a>, <a href="/wiki/Post-harvest_handling" title="Post-harvest handling" class="mw-redirect">post-harvest handling</a>, <a href="/wiki/Food_miles" title="Food miles">transport of food products</a>, <a href="/wiki/Packaging" title="Packaging" class="mw-redirect">packaging</a>, <a href="/wiki/Food_preservation" title="Food preservation">food preservation</a>, <a href="/wiki/Food_processing" title="Food processing">food processing</a>/value addition, <a href="/wiki/Quality_management" title="Quality management">quality management</a>, <a href="/wiki/Food_safety" title="Food safety">food safety</a>, <a href="/wiki/Food_storage" title="Food storage">food storage</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Food_marketing" title="Food marketing">food marketing</a>.</p> 
     <p>All stakeholders of agriculture industry need information and knowledge about these phases to manage them efficiently. Any system applied for getting information and knowledge for making decisions in any industry should deliver accurate, complete, concise information in time or on time. The information provided by the system must be in user-friendly form, easy to access, cost-effective and well protected from unauthorized accesses.</p> 
     <ul> 
      <li>Record text, drawings, photographs, audio, video, process descriptions, and other information in digital formats,</li> 
      <li>Produce exact duplicates of such information at significantly lower cost,</li> 
      <li>Transfer information and knowledge rapidly over large distances through communications networks.</li> 
      <li>Develop standardized algorithms to large quantities of information relatively rapidly.</li> 
      <li>Achieve greater interactivity in communicating, evaluating, producing and sharing useful information and knowledge.</li> 
     </ul> 
     <p>The main focus of this article is to elaborate how the achievements of IT can be applied in Agriculture sector and its development. The main applications of ICT in Agriculture sector are listed below.</p>

     <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Office_automation">Office automation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=ICT_in_agriculture&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Office automation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
     <p>The <a href="/wiki/Office_automation" title="Office automation">office automation</a> is application of computers, computer networks, telephone networks, and other office automation tool such as photocopy machines, scanners, printers, cleaning equipment, and electronic security systems to increase the productivity of organizations.</p> 
     <p>There are many government, private and non-government organizations involved in agriculture sector and rural development. They all have to work together to give better service to farming community. Therefore, application of office automation is one of the solutions to enhance the efficiency and inter-connectivity of the employees work in all above mentioned organizations.</p> 
     <p>Many computer applications such as <a href="/wiki/MS_Office" title="MS Office" class="mw-redirect">MS Office</a>, <a href="/wiki/Internet_Explorer" title="Internet Explorer">Internet Explorer</a>, <a href="/wiki/OpenOffice.org" title="OpenOffice.org">OpenOffice.org</a> and other tailor-made <a href="/wiki/Office_automation_software" title="Office automation software" class="mw-redirect">office automation software</a> packages are providing unlimited potential to organizations and individuals to fulfill their day to day data processing requirements to give an efficient service to their customers.</p> 
     <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Wireless_technologies">Wireless technologies</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=ICT_in_agriculture&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Wireless technologies">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
     <p><a href="/wiki/Wireless" title="Wireless">Wireless</a> technologies have numerous applications in agriculture.      <p>In agriculture, the use of the <a href="/wiki/Global_Positioning_System" title="Global Positioning System">Global Positioning System</a> provides benefits in <a href="/wiki/Geofencing" title="Geofencing" class="mw-redirect">geo-fencing</a>, <a href="/wiki/Map_making" title="Map making" class="mw-redirect">map-making</a> and <a href="/wiki/Surveying" title="Surveying">surveying</a>. GPS receivers dropped in price over the years, making it more popular for civilian use. With the use of GPS, civilians can produce simple yet highly accurate digitized map without the help of a professional <a href="/wiki/Cartographer" title="Cartographer" class="mw-redirect">cartographer</a>.</p> 
     <p>In <a href="/wiki/Kenya" title="Kenya">Kenya</a>, for example, the solution to prevent an elephant bull from wandering into farms and destroying precious crops was to tag the elephant with a device that sends a text message when it crosses a geo-fence. Using the technology of <a href="/wiki/SMS" title="SMS" class="mw-redirect">SMS</a> and GPS, the elephant can roam freely and the authorities are alerted whenever it is near the farm.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup></p>

     <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geographic_information_systems">Geographic information systems</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=ICT_in_agriculture&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Geographic information systems">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
     <p><a href="/wiki/Geographic_information_system" title="Geographic information system">Geographic information system</a> s, or GIS, are extensively used in agriculture, especially in <a href="/wiki/Precision_agriculture" title="Precision agriculture">precision farming</a>. Land is mapped digitally, and pertinent geodetic data such as topography and contours are combined with other statistical data for easier analysis of the soil. GIS is used in decision making such as what to plant and where to plant using historical data and sampling.</p>

     <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Computer-controlled_devices_.28automated_systems.29">Computer-controlled devices (automated systems)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=ICT_in_agriculture&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Computer-controlled devices (automated systems)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

     <p><a href="/wiki/Automatic_milking" title="Automatic milking">Automatic milking</a> systems are computer controlled stand alone systems that milk the dairy cattle without human labor. The complete automation of the milking process is controlled by an <a href="/wiki/Agricultural_robot" title="Agricultural robot">agricultural robot</a>, a complex herd management software, and specialized computers. Automatic milking eliminates the farmer from the actual milking process, allowing for more time for supervision of the farm and the herd. Farmers can also improve herd management by using the data gathered by the computer. By analyzing the effect of various <a href="/wiki/Fodder" title="Fodder">animal feeds</a> on milk yield, farmers may adjust accordingly to obtain optimal milk yields. Since the data is available down to individual level, each cow may be tracked and examined, and the farmer may be alerted when there are unusual changes that could mean sickness or injuries.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup></p>

     <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Smartphone_mobile_apps_in_Agriculture">Smartphone mobile apps in Agriculture</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=ICT_in_agriculture&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Smartphone mobile apps in Agriculture">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
     <p><a href="/wiki/E-learning" title="E-learning" class="mw-redirect">E-learning</a></p> 
     <table class="metadata plainlinks ambox mbox-small-left ambox-content" role="presentation">

     <p>Use of Mobile technologies as a tool of intervention in agriculture is increasingly popular. Smartphone penetration enhance the multi-dimensional positive impact on sustainable poverty reduction and identify accessibility as the main challenge in harnessing the full potential (Silarszky et al., 2008) in agricultural space. Reach of smart phone even in rural areas extended the ICT services beyond simple voice or text messages. Several smartphone apps are available for agriculture, horticulture, animal husbandry and farm machinery.</p> 
     <p>Smartphone mobile applications designed and developed by Jayalaxmi agrotech Pvt Ltd from India are the most commonly used agriculture apps in India. Their mobile apps are in regional language are designed to break the literacy barrier and deliver the information in most simple manner. Several thousands of farmers across Asia are empowered with these apps.</p> 
     <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="RFID">RFID</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=ICT_in_agriculture&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: RFID">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
     <div class="thumb tright"> 
      <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">
       <a href="/wiki/File:Swiss_cow_ear_with_eartags.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/95/Swiss_cow_ear_with_eartags.jpg/220px-Swiss_cow_ear_with_eartags.jpg" width="220" height="200" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/95/Swiss_cow_ear_with_eartags.jpg/330px-Swiss_cow_ear_with_eartags.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/95/Swiss_cow_ear_with_eartags.jpg/440px-Swiss_cow_ear_with_eartags.jpg 2x" data-file-width="4032" data-file-height="3660"></a> 
       <div class="thumbcaption"> 
        <div class="magnify">
         <a href="/wiki/File:Swiss_cow_ear_with_eartags.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a>
        </div> Swiss cow ear with eartag and RFID
       </div>

     <p>The Veterinary Department of <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://www.moa.gov.my/web/guest/home">Malaysia's Ministry of Agriculture</a> introduced a livestock-tracking program in 2009 to track the estimated 80,000 cattle all across the country. Each cattle is tagged with the use of <a href="/wiki/RFID" title="RFID" class="mw-redirect">RFID technology</a> for easier identification, providing access to relevant data such as: bearer's location, name of breeder, origin of livestock, sex, and dates of movement. This program is the first of its kind in Asia, and is expected to increase the competitiveness of Malaysian livestock industry in international markets by satisfying the regulatory requirements of importing countries like United States, Europe and Middle East. Tracking by RFID will also help producers meet the dietary standards by the <a href="/wiki/Halal" title="Halal">halal</a> market. The program will also provide improvements in controlling disease outbreaks in livestock.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5"><span>[</span>5<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
