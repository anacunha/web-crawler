     <p><b>3Degrees</b> is an organization based in the United States that works with energy utilities to offer "green power options" to customers by providing renewable energy certificates and carbon offsets.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> Its mission, as stated on its website, is to “connect people with renewable energy on a massive scale.”<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> The <a href="/wiki/U.S._Department_of_Energy" title="U.S. Department of Energy" class="mw-redirect">U.S. Department of Energy</a> and <a href="/wiki/U.S._Environmental_Protection_Agency" title="U.S. Environmental Protection Agency" class="mw-redirect">U.S. Environmental Protection Agency</a> has recognized 3Degrees with the Green Power Supplier of the Year Award multiple times.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> 3Degrees has been named #1 REC Trading Company in North America by Environmental Finance Magazine.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Identifying_reliable_sources" title="Wikipedia:Identifying reliable sources"><span title="The material near this tag may rely on an unreliable source. (April 2015)">unreliable source?</span></a></i>]</sup></p> 
     <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Overview">Overview</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=3Degrees&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Overview">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
     <p>3Degrees partners with organizations that seek to utilize environmental markets to build value for their customers, employees, investors and other stakeholders. 3Degrees helps these organizations buy, sell and market environmental commodities such as Renewable Energy Certificates (RECs) and verified carbon offsets.</p> 
     <p>3Degrees’ partners include utilities and Load-Serving Entities (LSEs), renewable energy project owners, institutions and non-profits.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> 3Degrees also works with green building professionals to help them use environmental commodities to offset the <a href="/wiki/Greenhouse_gas" title="Greenhouse gas">greenhouse gas</a> emissions associated with their buildings, allowing these buildings to earn points toward the U.S. Green Building Council’s (<a href="/wiki/USGBC" title="USGBC" class="mw-redirect">USGBC</a>) <a href="/wiki/Leadership_in_Energy_and_Environmental_Design" title="Leadership in Energy and Environmental Design">Leadership in Energy and Environmental Design</a> (LEED) certification.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> 3Degrees sources carbon offsets and RECs from hundreds of renewable energy and carbon reduction projects. Project types include solar, wind, <a href="/wiki/Landfill_gas" title="Landfill gas">landfill gas</a>, livestock <a href="/wiki/Biogas" title="Biogas">biogas</a>, <a href="/wiki/Geothermal_electricity" title="Geothermal electricity">geothermal</a>, <a href="/wiki/Biomass" title="Biomass">biomass</a>, low-impact hydro and <a href="/wiki/Sustainable_forestry" title="Sustainable forestry" class="mw-redirect">sustainable forestry</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> 3Degrees sells RECs that are Green-e Energy certified or eligible for compliance with state <a href="/wiki/Renewable_Portfolio_Standards" title="Renewable Portfolio Standards" class="mw-redirect">Renewable Portfolio Standards</a> (RPS).<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> 3Degrees also offers its partners carbon offsets which are verified using objective, credible and transparent carbon offset protocols and registries. Carbon offset protocols and registries used by 3Degrees include Green-e Climate, Climate Action Reserve (CAR), and the <a href="/wiki/Voluntary_Carbon_Standard" title="Voluntary Carbon Standard">Voluntary Carbon Standard</a> (VCS).<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> 3Degrees is a member of the <a href="/wiki/U.S._Green_Building_Council" title="U.S. Green Building Council">U.S. Green Building Council</a> (USGBC), and Renewable Energy Market Association (REMA).<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
