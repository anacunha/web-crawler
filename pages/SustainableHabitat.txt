   <h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">Sustainable habitat</h1> 
   <div id="bodyContent" class="mw-body-content">

    <div id="mw-content-text" lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr">
     <p>A <b>sustainable habitat</b> is an <a href="/wiki/Ecosystem" title="Ecosystem">ecosystem</a> that produces food and shelter for people and other organisms, without resource depletion and in such a way that no external waste is produced. Thus the habitat can continue into future tie without external infusions of resource. Such a sustainable habitat may evolve naturally or be produced under the influence of man.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (June 2012)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> A <a href="/wiki/Sustainability" title="Sustainability">sustainable</a> <a href="/wiki/Habitat" title="Habitat">habitat</a> that is created and designed by human intelligence will mimic nature, if it is to be successful. Everything within it is connected to a complex array of organisms, physical resources and functions. Organisms from many different <a href="/wiki/Biome" title="Biome">biomes</a> can be brought together to fulfill various ecological niches.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (June 2012)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p> 
     <p>The term often refers to sustainable <a href="/wiki/Category:Human_habitats" title="Category:Human habitats">human habitats</a>, which typically involve some form of <a href="/wiki/Green_building" title="Green building">green building</a> or <a href="/wiki/Environmental_planning" title="Environmental planning">environmental planning</a>.</p> 
     <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Overview">Overview</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sustainable_habitat&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Overview">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
     <p>In creating the sustainable habitats, <a href="/wiki/Environmental_scientist" title="Environmental scientist" class="mw-redirect">environmental scientists</a>, designers, engineers and <a href="/wiki/Architecture" title="Architecture">architects</a> must consider no element as a <a href="/wiki/Waste" title="Waste">waste</a> product to be disposed of somewhere off site, but as a nutrient stream for another process to feed on. Researching ways to interconnect waste streams to production creates a more sustainable society by minimizing <a href="/wiki/Pollution" title="Pollution">pollution</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (June 2012)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
