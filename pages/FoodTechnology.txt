   <h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">Food technology</h1>

     <p><b>Food Technology</b> is a branch of <a href="/wiki/Food_science" title="Food science">food science</a> that deals with the production processes that <a href="/wiki/Food_manufacture" title="Food manufacture" class="mw-redirect">make foods</a>.</p> 
     <p>Early <a href="/wiki/Food_science" title="Food science">scientific research</a> into food technology concentrated on <a href="/wiki/Food_preservation" title="Food preservation">food preservation</a>. <a href="/wiki/Nicolas_Appert" title="Nicolas Appert">Nicolas Appert</a>’s development in 1810 of the <a href="/wiki/Canning" title="Canning">canning</a> process was a decisive event. The process wasn’t called canning then and Appert did not really know the principle on which his process worked, but canning has had a major impact on food preservation techniques.</p> 
     <p><a href="/wiki/Louis_Pasteur" title="Louis Pasteur">Louis Pasteur</a>'s research on the spoilage of <a href="/wiki/Wine" title="Wine">wine</a> and his description of how to avoid spoilage in 1864 was an early attempt to apply scientific knowledge to food handling. Besides research into <a href="/wiki/Wine_spoilage" title="Wine spoilage" class="mw-redirect">wine spoilage</a>, Pasteur researched the production of <a href="/wiki/Alcohol" title="Alcohol">alcohol</a>, <a href="/wiki/Vinegar" title="Vinegar">vinegar</a>, wines and <a href="/wiki/Beer" title="Beer">beer</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/Soured_milk" title="Soured milk">souring of milk</a>. He developed <a href="/wiki/Pasteurization" title="Pasteurization">pasteurization</a>—the process of heating milk and milk products to destroy food spoilage and disease-producing <a href="/wiki/Microorganisms" title="Microorganisms" class="mw-redirect">organisms</a>. In his research into food technology, Pasteur became the pioneer into <a href="/wiki/Bacteria" title="Bacteria">bacteriology</a> and of modern <a href="/wiki/Preventive_healthcare" title="Preventive healthcare">preventive medicine</a>.</p>

     <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Developments">Developments</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Food_technology&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Developments">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
     <div class="thumb tright"> 
      <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">
       <a href="/wiki/File:Liofilizat_04_pl.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/89/Liofilizat_04_pl.jpg/220px-Liofilizat_04_pl.jpg" width="220" height="147" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/89/Liofilizat_04_pl.jpg/330px-Liofilizat_04_pl.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/89/Liofilizat_04_pl.jpg/440px-Liofilizat_04_pl.jpg 2x" data-file-width="2700" data-file-height="1800"></a> 
       <div class="thumbcaption"> 
        <div class="magnify">
         <a href="/wiki/File:Liofilizat_04_pl.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a>
        </div> 
        <a href="/wiki/Freeze-drying" title="Freeze-drying">Freeze-dried</a> 
        <a href="/wiki/Coffee" title="Coffee">coffee</a>, a form of 
        <a href="/wiki/Instant_coffee" title="Instant coffee">instant coffee</a>
       </div> 
      </div> 
     </div> 
     <p>Developments in food technology have contributed greatly to the food supply and have changed our world. Some of these developments are:</p> 
     <ul> 
      <li><a href="/wiki/Powdered_milk" title="Powdered milk">Instantized Milk Powder</a> - D.D. Peebles (U.S. patent 2,835,586) developed the first instant milk powder, which has become the basis for a variety of new products that are rehydratable. This process increases the <a href="/wiki/Surface_area" title="Surface area">surface area</a> of the powdered product by partially rehydrating spray-dried milk powder.</li> 
      <li><a href="/wiki/Freeze-drying" title="Freeze-drying">Freeze-drying</a> - The first application of freeze drying was most likely in the <a href="/wiki/Pharmaceutical" title="Pharmaceutical" class="mw-redirect">pharmaceutical</a> industry; however, a successful large-scale industrial application of the process was the development of continuous freeze drying of coffee.</li> 
      <li><a href="/wiki/Ultra-high_temperature_processing" title="Ultra-high temperature processing" class="mw-redirect">High-Temperature Short Time Processing</a> - These processes for the most part are characterized by rapid heating and cooling, holding for a short time at a relatively high temperature and filling aseptically into <a href="/wiki/Sterilization_(microbiology)" title="Sterilization (microbiology)">sterile</a> containers.</li> 
      <li><a href="/wiki/Decaffeination" title="Decaffeination">Decaffeination</a> of <a href="/wiki/Coffee" title="Coffee">Coffee</a> and <a href="/wiki/Tea" title="Tea">Tea</a> - Decaffeinated coffee and tea was first developed on a commercial basis in <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a> around 1900. The process is described in U.S. patent 897,763. Green coffee beans are treated with water, heat and <a href="/wiki/Solvent" title="Solvent">solvents</a> to remove the caffeine from the beans.</li> 
      <li><a href="/wiki/Process_optimization" title="Process optimization">Process optimization</a> - Food Technology now allows production of foods to be more efficient, Oil saving technologies are now available on different forms. Production methods and <a href="/wiki/Methodology" title="Methodology">methodology</a> have also become increasingly sophisticated.</li> 
     </ul> 
     <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Consumer_acceptance">Consumer acceptance</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Food_technology&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Consumer acceptance">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
     <p>In the past, consumer attitude towards food technologies was not common talk and was not important in food development. Nowadays the <a href="/wiki/Food_chain" title="Food chain">food chain</a> is long and complicated, foods and food technologies are diverse; consequently the consumers are uncertain about the food quality and safety and find it difficult to orient themselves to the subject. That is why consumer acceptance of food technologies is an important question. However, in these days acceptance of food products very often depends on potential benefits and risks associated with the food. This also includes the technology the food is processed with. Attributes like “uncertain”, “unknown” or “unfamiliar” are associated with consumers’ <a href="/wiki/Risk_perception" title="Risk perception">risk perception</a> and consumer very likely will reject products linked to these attributes. Especially innovative food processing technologies are connected to these characteristics and are perceived as risky by consumers <sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup></p> 
     <p>Acceptance of the different food technologies is very different. Whereas pasteurisation is well recognised, high pressure treatment or even microwaves are perceived as risky very often. In studies done within Hightech Europe project, it was found that traditional technologies were well accepted in contrast to innovative technologies.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup></p> 
     <p>Consumers form their attitude towards innovative food technologies by three main factors mechanisms. First, knowledge or beliefs about risks and benefits which are correlated with the technology. Second, attitudes are based on their own experience and third, based on higher order values and beliefs.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup></p> 
     <p>Acceptance of innovative technologies can be improved by providing non-emotional and concise information about these new technological processes methods. According to a study made by HighTech project also written information seems to have higher impact than audio-visual information on the consumer in case of sensory acceptance of products processed with innovative food technologies.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
