   <h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">Borehole</h1>

     <p>A <b>borehole</b> is a narrow <a href="/wiki/Shaft_mining" title="Shaft mining">shaft</a> <a href="/wiki/Boring_(earth)" title="Boring (earth)">bored</a> in the ground, either vertically or horizontally. A borehole may be constructed for many different purposes, including the extraction of <a href="/wiki/Water" title="Water">water</a>, other liquids (such as <a href="/wiki/Petroleum" title="Petroleum">petroleum</a>) or gases (such as <a href="/wiki/Natural_gas" title="Natural gas">natural gas</a>), as part of a <a href="/wiki/Geotechnical_investigation" title="Geotechnical investigation">geotechnical investigation</a>, <a href="/wiki/Phase_I_Environmental_Site_Assessment#Other_types_of_ESA" title="Phase I Environmental Site Assessment" class="mw-redirect">environmental site assessment</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mineral_exploration" title="Mineral exploration">mineral exploration</a>, <a href="/wiki/Temperature" title="Temperature">temperature</a> measurement, as a pilot hole for installing piers or underground utilities, for geothermal installations, or for underground storage of unwanted substances, e.g. in <a href="/wiki/Carbon_capture_and_storage" title="Carbon capture and storage">Carbon capture and storage</a>.</p>

     <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Uses">Uses</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Borehole&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Uses">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
     <div class="thumb tright"> 
      <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">
       <a href="/wiki/File:AlburyBoreHole.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c9/AlburyBoreHole.jpg/220px-AlburyBoreHole.jpg" width="220" height="147" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c9/AlburyBoreHole.jpg/330px-AlburyBoreHole.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c9/AlburyBoreHole.jpg/440px-AlburyBoreHole.jpg 2x" data-file-width="3072" data-file-height="2048"></a> 
       <div class="thumbcaption"> 
        <div class="magnify">
         <a href="/wiki/File:AlburyBoreHole.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a>
        </div> A water borehole into the 
        <a href="/wiki/Chalk" title="Chalk">chalk</a> 
        <a href="/wiki/Aquifer" title="Aquifer">aquifer</a> under the 
        <a href="/wiki/North_Downs" title="North Downs">North Downs</a>, England at 
        <a href="/wiki/Albury,_Surrey" title="Albury, Surrey">Albury</a>
       </div> 
      </div> 
     </div> 
     <p><a href="/wiki/Engineering" title="Engineering">Engineers</a> and <a href="/wiki/Environmental_consulting" title="Environmental consulting">environmental consultants</a> use the term <i>borehole</i> to collectively describe all of the various types of holes drilled as part of a <a href="/wiki/Geotechnical_investigation" title="Geotechnical investigation">geotechnical investigation</a> or environmental site assessment (a so-called Phase II ESA). This includes holes advanced to collect soil samples, water samples or rock cores, to advance <i><a href="/wiki/In_situ" title="In situ">in situ</a></i> sampling equipment, or to install <a href="/wiki/Monitoring_well" title="Monitoring well" class="mw-redirect">monitoring wells</a> or <a href="/wiki/Piezometer" title="Piezometer">piezometers</a>. Samples collected from boreholes are often tested in a laboratory to determine their physical properties, or to assess levels of various chemical constituents or contaminants.</p> 
     <p>Typically, a borehole used as a <a href="/wiki/Water_well" title="Water well">water well</a> is completed by installing a vertical pipe (casing) and well screen to keep the borehole from caving. This also helps prevent surface <a href="/wiki/Contaminants" title="Contaminants" class="mw-redirect">contaminants</a> from entering the borehole and protects any installed pump from drawing in sand and sediment. <a href="/wiki/Oil_well" title="Oil well">Oil and natural gas wells</a> are completed in a similar, albeit usually more complex, manner.</p> 
     <p>As detailed in <a href="/wiki/Proxy_(climate)#Boreholes" title="Proxy (climate)">proxy (climate)</a>, borehole temperature measurements at a series of different depths can be effectively "<a href="/wiki/Invertible_matrix" title="Invertible matrix">inverted</a>" (a mathematical formula to solve a matrix equation) to help estimate historic surface temperatures.</p> 
     <p>Clusters of small-diameter boreholes equipped with heat exhangers made of plastic PEX pipe can be used to store heat or cold between opposing seasons in a mass of native rock. The technique is called <a href="/wiki/Seasonal_thermal_energy_storage" title="Seasonal thermal energy storage">seasonal thermal energy storage</a>. Mediums that can be used for this technique range from gravel to bedrock. There can be a few to several hundred boreholes, and in practice, depths have ranged from 150 to 1000 feet.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup></p> 
     <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Borehole&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
     <p>Borehole drilling has a long history. <a href="/wiki/Han_Dynasty" title="Han Dynasty" class="mw-redirect">Han Dynasty</a> China (202 BC&nbsp;– 220 AD) used deep borehole drilling for mining and other projects. Chinese borehole sites could reach as deep as 600&nbsp;m (2000&nbsp;ft).<sup id="cite_ref-loewe_1968_194_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-loewe_1968_194-3"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup> The practice of <a href="/wiki/Well_logging" title="Well logging">well logging</a> in boreholes dates to 1927, for the French <a href="/wiki/Pechelbronn" title="Pechelbronn" class="mw-redirect">Pechelbronn</a> oil field.<sup id="cite_ref-Telford_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Telford-4"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup></p> 
     <p>For many years, the world’s deepest borehole was the <a href="/wiki/Kola_Superdeep_Borehole" title="Kola Superdeep Borehole">Kola Superdeep Borehole</a>. From 2011 until August 2012 the record was held by the 12,345-metre (40,502&nbsp;ft) long <a href="/wiki/Sakhalin-I" title="Sakhalin-I">Sakhalin-I</a> Odoptu OP-11 Well, offshore the Russian island <a href="/wiki/Sakhalin" title="Sakhalin">Sakhalin</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5"><span>[</span>5<span>]</span></a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Sakhalin-I#Fields:_Chayvo.2C_Odoptu_and_Arkutun-Dagi" title="Sakhalin-I">Chayvo</a> Z-44 extended-reach well took the title of the world's longest borehole on 27 August 2012. Z-44's total measured depth is 12,376&nbsp;m (40,604&nbsp;ft). However, ERD wells are more shallow than Kola Superdeep Borehole, owing to a large horizontal displacement.</p> 
     <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Methodology">Methodology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Borehole&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Methodology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
     <p>Drillers may sink a borehole using a <a href="/wiki/Drilling_rig" title="Drilling rig">drilling rig</a> or a hand-operated rig. The machinery and techniques to advance a borehole vary considerably according to manufacturer, geological conditions, and the intended purpose. For <a href="/wiki/Offshore_drilling" title="Offshore drilling">offshore drilling</a> floating units or platforms supported by the seafloor are used for the drilling rig.</p>
