     <p>The <b>Fab Tree Hab</b> is a hypothetical ecological home design developed at <a href="/wiki/MIT" title="MIT" class="mw-redirect">MIT</a> by <a href="/wiki/Mitchell_Joachim" title="Mitchell Joachim">Mitchell Joachim</a>, Javier Arbona and Lara Greden. With the idea of easing the burden Humanity places on the environment with conventional housing by growing "living, breathing" <a href="/wiki/Tree_house" title="Tree house">tree homes</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-CanadaFab_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CanadaFab-2"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup></p> 
     <p>It would be built by allowing native trees to grow over a computer-designed (<a href="/wiki/CNC" title="CNC" class="mw-redirect">CNC</a>) removable <a href="/wiki/Plywood" title="Plywood">plywood</a> <a href="/wiki/Scaffold" title="Scaffold" class="mw-redirect">scaffold</a>. Once the plants are interconnected and stable,<sup id="cite_ref-TrerreformOne_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TrerreformOne-1"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup> the plywood would be removed and reused. MIT is experimenting with <a href="/wiki/Trees" title="Trees" class="mw-redirect">trees</a> that grow quickly and develop an interwoven root structure that's soft enough to "train" over the scaffold, but then hardens into a more durable structure. The inside walls would be conventional <a href="/wiki/Clay" title="Clay">clay</a> and <a href="/wiki/Plaster" title="Plaster">plaster</a>.</p> 
     <p>An old methodology new to buildings is introduced in this design - <a href="/wiki/Pleaching" title="Pleaching">pleaching</a>. Pleaching is a method of weaving together tree branches to form living <a href="/wiki/Arch" title="Arch">archways</a>, <a href="/wiki/Latticework" title="Latticework">lattices</a>, or screens. <sup id="cite_ref-Technologyreview_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Technologyreview-3"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup> One of the companies giving advice on the use of <a href="/wiki/Tree_shaping#Aeroponic_culture" title="Tree shaping">Aeroponic culture</a> with pleaching is <a href="/w/index.php?title=Treenovations&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Treenovations (page does not exist)">Plantware</a><sup id="cite_ref-CanadaFab_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CanadaFab-2"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup> The load-bearing part of the structure is to use trees that self-graft or inosculate such as <a href="/wiki/Live_oak" title="Live oak">Live Oak</a>, <a href="/wiki/Elm" title="Elm">Elm</a> and <a href="/wiki/Dogwood" title="Dogwood" class="mw-redirect">Dogwood</a>. The lattice frame for the walls and roof are created with the branches of the trees. Using vines to create a dense protective layer woven along the exterior, interspersed with soil pockets and growing plants. To increase the control, depth and accuracy of this building method a conventional computer designs the scaffold use to grow the plants on. <sup id="cite_ref-TrerreformOne_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TrerreformOne-1"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup></p> 
     <p>The Fab Tree Hab is an experiment that would develop over time. Extra operating costs required over the life-time of the home include pest management with organic pesticides and maintenance of the <a href="/wiki/Living_machine" title="Living machine">living machine</a>'s water treatment system. Technical demonstration and innovation is still needed for certain components, primarily the bioplastic windows that accept growth of the structure and the management of flows across the wall section to assure that the interior remains dry and animal-free. All in all, the elapsed time to reach livability is greater than the traditional sense, but so should be the health and longevity of the home and family. Above all, building this home could be achieved at a minimal price. Depending on the surrounding climate the house is to be grown in, the team expect it will take a minimum of five years to complete its structure.<sup id="cite_ref-CanadaFab_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CanadaFab-2"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup> <sup id="cite_ref-TrerreformOne_1-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TrerreformOne-1"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup>Realization of these homes will begin as an experiment, and it is envisioned that thereafter, the concept of renewal will take on a new architectural form - one of inter-dependency between nature and people.</p> 
     <p>As of May 2007 Mitchell Joachim stated that there is a "50 per cent" organic project in California. Combining natural elements and traditional construction. <sup id="cite_ref-CanadaFab_2-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CanadaFab-2"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
