     <p><b>Charles Gordon-Lennox, Earl of March and Kinrara</b> <a href="/wiki/Deputy_Lieutenant" title="Deputy Lieutenant">DL</a> (born 8 January 1955) is a British aristocrat, eldest son and heir-apparent of the <a href="/wiki/Charles_Gordon-Lennox,_10th_Duke_of_Richmond" title="Charles Gordon-Lennox, 10th Duke of Richmond">Duke of Richmond</a>, and owner of <a href="/wiki/Goodwood_House" title="Goodwood House">Goodwood Estate</a> in West Sussex.<sup id="cite_ref-Independent2009_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Independent2009-1">[1]</a></sup> He is the founder of the <a href="/wiki/Goodwood_Festival_of_Speed" title="Goodwood Festival of Speed">Goodwood Festival of Speed</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Goodwood_Revival" title="Goodwood Revival">Goodwood Revival</a>.</p> 
     <p>He is President of the British Automobile Racing Club, Patron of the TT Riders Association and an honorary member of the British Racing Drivers Club, the Guild of Motoring Writers and the 500 Owners Club.</p>

     <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Photography">Photography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Charles_Gordon-Lennox,_Earl_of_March_and_Kinrara&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Photography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
     <p>Having had a passion for film and photography since the age of 10, March left <a href="/wiki/Eton_College" title="Eton College">Eton College</a> at the first possible opportunity and at 17 worked for the film director <a href="/wiki/Stanley_Kubrick" title="Stanley Kubrick">Stanley Kubrick</a> on the film <i><a href="/wiki/Barry_Lyndon" title="Barry Lyndon">Barry Lyndon</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-Independent2009_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Independent2009-1">[1]</a></sup></p> 
     <p>In 2012, a major exhibition of March’s recent photographic work, ‘Nature Translated’, was staged at the Bermondsey Project Space in London.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (January 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> The exhibition was shown at the Marble Palace, part of the State Russian Museum in St Petersburg, in January 2014 and in Moscow as part of the Moscow Photography Biennale in April 2014.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (January 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> Two new exhibitions of March’s photographs were held in early 2015: ‘Wood Land’ which was held at Venus Over Manhattan Gallery in New York City, and ‘Abstract and Intentional’ which was held at Hamiltons Gallery in London.</p> 
     <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Goodwood">Goodwood</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Charles_Gordon-Lennox,_Earl_of_March_and_Kinrara&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Goodwood">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
     <div role="note" class="hatnote relarticle mainarticle">
      Main article: 
      <a href="/wiki/Goodwood_House" title="Goodwood House">Goodwood House</a>
     </div> 
     <p>March moved from London to the family seat Goodwood to take over management of the estate, following the family tradition of the duke handing over management of the estate to the heir apparent when the latter turns forty.<sup id="cite_ref-Independent2009_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Independent2009-1">[1]</a></sup></p> 
     <p>Motor sport at Goodwood was started by his grandfather, <a href="/wiki/Frederick_Gordon-Lennox,_9th_Duke_of_Richmond" title="Frederick Gordon-Lennox, 9th Duke of Richmond">Freddie Richmond</a>, who opened the Goodwood Motor Circuit in 1948. March established the Festival of Speed at Goodwood House in 1993.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> He then brought motor racing back to the circuit, which had closed in 1966, with the creation of the Goodwood Revival in 1998. Both events have since become recognised<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="margin-left:0.1em; white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Manual_of_Style/Words_to_watch#Unsupported_attributions" title="Wikipedia:Manual of Style/Words to watch"><span title="The material near this tag may use weasel words or too-vague attribution. (January 2015)">according to whom?</span></a></i>]</sup> as some of the most unusual, exciting and creative events in the world.</p> 
     <p>The Goodwood Estate covers 12,000 acres to the north of Chichester. The Goodwood Estate Company is a diverse portfolio of businesses which includes: Goodwood Racecourse, a 4,000 acre organic farm, two eighteen hole golf courses, Goodwood Aerodrome and Flying School, and a 91 bedroom hotel. The Group employs over 550 people and attracts 800,000 visitors to the Estate each year.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (January 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> The headquarters of <a href="/wiki/Rolls_Royce_Motor_Cars" title="Rolls Royce Motor Cars" class="mw-redirect">Rolls Royce Motor Cars</a> is also on the Estate.</p> 
     <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Family">Family</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Charles_Gordon-Lennox,_Earl_of_March_and_Kinrara&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Family">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
     <p>March lives in Goodwood House with his wife, Janet (née Astor), and their four children.</p> 
     <p>March has been married twice, and has three sons and two daughters:</p> 
     <ul> 
      <li>1) Sally Clayton (1976 to 1989); one daughter 
       <ul> 
        <li>I) Lady Alexandra Gordon-Lennox (born 1985)</li> 
       </ul> </li> 
      <li>2) The Honourable Janet Elizabeth Astor, daughter of the <a href="/wiki/William_Astor,_3rd_Viscount_Astor" title="William Astor, 3rd Viscount Astor">3rd Viscount Astor</a> (1991 to date); one daughter and three sons 
       <ul> 
        <li>II) Charles Henry Gordon-Lennox, Lord Settrington (born 20 December 1994), is the Earl's <a href="/wiki/Heir_apparent" title="Heir apparent">heir apparent</a></li> 
        <li>III) The Hon. William Rupert Gordon-Lennox (born 29 November 1996)</li> 
        <li>IV) Lady Eloise Cordelia Gordon-Lennox (born 10 March 2000)</li> 
        <li>V) The Hon. Frederick Lysander Gordon-Lennox (born 10 March 2000)</li> 
       </ul> </li> 
     </ul> 
     <p>In January 2016 he and Lady March were attacked and tied-up in a major jewel robbery at Goodwood.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
