     <p>The <b>Nano House</b> was named to be the world's smallest sustainable house.</p> 
     <p>The Nano Living System is a Swiss made "<a href="/wiki/Green_building" title="Green building">green</a>" pre-engineered concept for residential use that presents an innovative and sustainable architectural proposition. The design was based on a combination of pre-engineered <a href="/wiki/Structural_insulated_panel" title="Structural insulated panel">SIPs</a> (made from cement fiberboard, recycled light gauge steel and <a href="/wiki/Polyurethane" title="Polyurethane">polyurethane</a> foam), creation of flexible spaces (through Nano Living Systems' "Suspending Technology") and use of <a href="/wiki/Renewable_energy" title="Renewable energy">renewable energy</a> systems. This suggests an optimistic and environmental solution for the global housing issue of very small living spaces at extremely high prices.</p> 
     <p>The "Nano House" can be used by a family of three in an area consisting of 25 square metres (270&nbsp;sq&nbsp;ft). This is made possible by the incorporation of the "suspending technology", which nearly doubles the size of the living area within this space by transforming what is common living space by day into two separate bedrooms by night. The "suspending technology" can be used in new construction and can also be adapted to be used in existing structures, such as hotels, studios, dormitories and very small housing.</p>
