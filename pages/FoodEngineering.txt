     <p><b>Food engineering</b> is a multidisciplinary field of applied <a href="/wiki/Physical_sciences" title="Physical sciences" class="mw-redirect">physical sciences</a> which combines science, <a href="/wiki/Food_microbiology" title="Food microbiology">microbiology</a>, and engineering education for food and related industries. Food engineering includes, but is not limited to, the application of <a href="/wiki/Agricultural_engineering" title="Agricultural engineering">agricultural engineering</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mechanical_engineering" title="Mechanical engineering">mechanical engineering</a> and <a href="/wiki/Chemical_engineering" title="Chemical engineering">chemical engineering</a> principles to food materials. <a href="/w/index.php?title=Food_engineers&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Food engineers (page does not exist)">Food engineers</a> provide the technological knowledge transfer essential to the <a href="/wiki/Cost-effective" title="Cost-effective" class="mw-redirect">cost-effective</a> production and <a href="/wiki/Commercialization" title="Commercialization">commercialization</a> of food products and services. Physics, chemistry, and mathematics are fundamental to understanding and engineering products and operations in the <a href="/wiki/Food_industry" title="Food industry">food industry</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-intro_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-intro-1"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup></p> 
     <p>Food engineering encompasses a wide range of activities. Food engineers are employed in food processing, food machinery, <a href="/wiki/Packaging" title="Packaging" class="mw-redirect">packaging</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ingredient" title="Ingredient">ingredient</a> manufacturing, instrumentation, and control. Firms that design and build food processing plants, consulting firms, government agencies, <a href="/wiki/Pharmaceutical" title="Pharmaceutical" class="mw-redirect">pharmaceutical</a> companies, and <a href="/wiki/Health-care" title="Health-care" class="mw-redirect">health-care</a> firms also employ food engineers. Specific food engineering activities include:</p> 
     <ul> 
      <li>research and development of new foods, biological and pharmaceutical products;</li> 
      <li>development and operation of manufacturing, packaging and distributing systems for drug/food products;</li> 
      <li>design and installation of food/biological/pharmaceutical production processes;</li> 
      <li>design and operation of environmentally responsible waste treatment systems;</li> 
      <li>marketing and technical support for manufacturing plants.</li> 
     </ul> 
     <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Topics_in_food_engineering">Topics in food engineering</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Food_engineering&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Topics in food engineering">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
     <p>In the development of food engineering, one of the many challenges is to employ modern tools, technology, and knowledge, such as computational materials science and <a href="/wiki/Nanotechnology" title="Nanotechnology">nanotechnology</a>, to develop new products and processes. Simultaneously, improving quality, safety, and security remain critical issues in food engineering study. New packaging materials and techniques are being developed to provide more protection to foods, and novel preservation technology is emerging. Additionally, process control and automation regularly appear among the top priorities identified in food engineering. Advanced monitoring and control systems are developed to facilitate automation and flexible food manufacturing. Furthermore, <a href="/wiki/Energy_saving" title="Energy saving" class="mw-redirect">energy saving</a> and minimization of environmental problems continue to be important food engineering issues, and significant progress is being made in <a href="/wiki/Waste_management" title="Waste management">waste management</a>, efficient utilization of energy, and reduction of effluents and emissions in food production.</p> 
     <p>Typical topics include:</p> 
     <ul> 
      <li>Advances in classical unit operations in engineering applied to food manufacturing</li> 
      <li>Progresses in the transport and <a href="/wiki/Food_storage" title="Food storage">storage</a> of liquid and solid foods</li> 
      <li>Developments in heating, chilling and <a href="/w/index.php?title=Frozen_foode&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Frozen foode (page does not exist)">freezing</a> of foods</li> 
      <li>Advanced mass transfer in foods</li> 
      <li>New chemical and biochemical aspects of food engineering and the use of kinetic analysis</li> 
      <li>New techniques in dehydration, thermal processing, non-thermal processing, <a href="/wiki/Food_extrusion" title="Food extrusion">extrusion</a>, liquid food concentration, membrane processes and applications of membranes in food processing</li> 
      <li>Shelf-life, electronic indicators in <a href="/wiki/Inventory_management" title="Inventory management" class="mw-redirect">inventory management</a>, and sustainable technologies in food processing</li> 
      <li>Modern <a href="/wiki/Food_packaging" title="Food packaging">packaging</a>, cleaning, and sanitation technologies.</li> 
     </ul>
