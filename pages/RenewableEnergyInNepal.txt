     <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solar_energy">Solar energy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Renewable_energy_in_Nepal&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Solar energy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
     <p>Power cuts with an average of 10 hours per day<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup> have been common in Nepal and Nepal Electricity Authority<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup> publishes a time table for power cuts. <a href="/wiki/Solar_energy" title="Solar energy">Solar energy</a> can be seen as a more reliable source of energy in Nepal than the traditional electricity. Private installations of solar panels are more frequent in Nepal. The US Embassy has installed the solar panels.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5"><span>[</span>5<span>]</span></a></sup></p> 
     <p>Places such as Madi, Chitwan where the Electricity Authority do not provide electricity<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6"><span>[</span>6<span>]</span></a></sup> because of <a href="/wiki/Chitwan_National_Park" title="Chitwan National Park">Chitwan National Park</a>, people there have been relying on solar power<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7"><span>[</span>7<span>]</span></a></sup> for several years.</p> 
     <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Electric_vehicles">Electric vehicles</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Renewable_energy_in_Nepal&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Electric vehicles">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
     <p>Electric powered<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8"><span>[</span>8<span>]</span></a></sup> public three wheeler have been in use to reduce <a href="/wiki/Carbon_dioxide" title="Carbon dioxide">carbon dioxide (CO<sub>2</sub>)</a> emissions. <sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9"><span>[</span>9<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
