     <p>An <b>energy-plus house</b> (also called: Plus-Energy House, Efficiency-Plus House) produces more energy from <a href="/wiki/Renewable_energy" title="Renewable energy">renewable energy</a> sources, over the course of a year, than it imports from external sources. This is achieved using a combination of <a href="/wiki/Microgeneration" title="Microgeneration">microgeneration</a> technology and <a href="/wiki/Low-energy_building" title="Low-energy building" class="mw-redirect">low-energy building</a> techniques, such as: <a href="/wiki/Passive_solar_building_design" title="Passive solar building design">passive solar building design</a>, <a href="/wiki/Building_insulation" title="Building insulation">insulation</a> and careful site selection and placement. A reduction of <a href="/wiki/Modern_conveniences" title="Modern conveniences" class="mw-redirect">modern conveniences</a> can also contribute to energy savings, however many energy-plus houses are almost indistinguishable from a traditional home, preferring instead to use highly energy-efficient appliances, fixtures, etc., throughout the house.</p>
